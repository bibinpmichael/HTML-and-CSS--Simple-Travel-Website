About Kuttanadu

Nestled in the heart of Kerala, Kuttanadu is a captivating destination renowned for its enchanting backwaters, verdant paddy fields, and distinctive agricultural practices. Known as the "Rice Bowl of Kerala," this region is one of the few places globally where farming is conducted below sea level, creating a stunning landscape of interconnected rivers, lakes, and canals. The serene and picturesque environment makes Kuttanadu an ideal spot for a peaceful retreat. Visitors can embark on memorable houseboat cruises, or "kettuvallams," offering a unique way to explore the backwaters while enjoying modern comforts. The local cuisine, celebrated for its traditional Kerala dishes, highlights fresh seafood and coconut-based specialties, providing a true taste of the region's culinary delights. Kuttanadu's rich cultural tapestry is woven with vibrant festivals, traditional boat races, and engaging folklore, with the Nehru Trophy Boat Race standing out as a major attraction that draws visitors from across the globe. Additionally, the region's diverse flora and fauna make it a paradise for nature lovers and bird watchers. Whether seeking relaxation amidst nature, immersion in local culture, or an adventurous houseboat journey, Kuttanadu offers an unforgettable experience that beautifully captures the essence of Kerala.

Top three activities to do at Kuttanadu

Cruise on a Houseboat

Experience the tranquility of Kuttanadu's backwaters by taking a houseboat cruise. Enjoy the scenic beauty, spot local wildlife, and relish traditional Kerala cuisine on board.

Witness a Snake Boat Race

Attend the thrilling Nehru Trophy Boat Race on Punnamada Lake, where long snake boats, rowed by hundreds of oarsmen, compete fiercely. It's a spectacle of strength and tradition.

Visit a Traditional Village

Explore a traditional Kuttanadu village to experience the local lifestyle, visit paddy fields, and interact with friendly locals. This provides a deeper insight into the region's rich cultural heritage.
